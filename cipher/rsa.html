RSA Explained

E (Public Exponent): A small exponent used for encryption.

N (Modulus): The product of two large primes, used in both keys.

D (Private Exponent): Computed as the modular inverse of E modulo φ(N), used for decryption.

How to Get D: Compute φ(N) = (p–1)(q–1) for primes p and q, then find D such that (D × E) mod φ(N) = 1.

Additional Info: RSA’s security relies on the difficulty of factoring N. Use large, random primes for robust security.
