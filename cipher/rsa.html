RSA Explained

What is E? E is the public exponent – a small, easy-to-use number that helps encrypt your message.

What is N (Modulus)? N is a big number created by multiplying two secret prime numbers. It’s part of both your public and private keys.

What is D? D is the private exponent – your secret decryption key that lets you unlock messages encrypted with E and N.

How do I get D? First, choose two large prime numbers and multiply them to get N. Then calculate φ(N) by multiplying one less than each prime. Finally, find D so that (D × E) mod φ(N) equals 1. D is your secret key!

Why is RSA Secure? RSA’s security comes from the fact that it’s extremely hard to factor the large number N back into its two prime numbers. Without these primes, figuring out D is nearly impossible.

Remember: Your public key (E, N) can be shared openly, but D must stay private – like a lock that anyone can close, but only you can open!
