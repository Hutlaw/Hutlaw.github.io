AES Explained

AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) is a symmetric encryption algorithm. In this demo we use AES-GCM mode with a 128-bit key (16 characters).

A random 12-byte initialization vector (IV) is generated during encryption and prepended to the ciphertext. During decryption, the IV is extracted and used to restore the original message.

Ensure your key is exactly 16 characters long. You can generate a random key with the button above.
